Steve Jobs

The man who changed the world

Steve Jobs (1955-2011), founder of Apple Inc.

Here's a timeline of Steve Jobs' life:

  • 1955 - born in San Francisco, California, SUA.
  • 1977 - released Apple II, which was one of the first highly successful mass-produced personal computers.
  • 1984 - released Macintosh, which was the first commercially successful PC to use a mouse and graphical user interface.
  • 1986 - funded and led Pixar Animation Studios, by buying an unproven animation studio from George Lucas.
  • 2001 - released iPod, a portable digital music player, which dominated the market.
  • 2001 - launched a chain of Apple retail stores, proving to be a huge success. Apple now operates more than 350 stores.
  • 2007 - released iPhone, a device that merged an iPod, full Internet browser and mobile phone into a gadget with multitouch-screen interface.
  • 2008 - launched App Store, a software used to download applications for iPhone and iPod Touch. He gave developers a way to write and sell mobile programs to consumers online.
  • 2010 - released iPad tablet, which, despite the doubters, became another hit product.
  • 2011 - dies at the age of 56, pancreatic cancer being the cause of death.
